# Context

- Trying to wrap the idea around an ayah of the Quran, using an opportunity to remember the word of Allah.
- This is aspired by Surah Ibrahim, 14th chapter of the Quran.
- The chapter belongs to the set of Surahs that deal with a similar subject.
- Deals with the latter life of the Prophet. When his teachings brought shock, and later things started getting worse and the harm to Muslims was becoming physical.
- This particular set of surahs was intended to help Muslims cope during difficult times. It's Allah’s way of encouraging them. Just as we need someone to encourage us in hard times, Allah encourages us in hard times.

## Surah Ibrahim, Ayah 1

- From the very beginning of this surah, the first thing mentioned in the surah is Allah mentioning to the Prophet: الٓر ۚ كِتَـٰبٌ أَنزَلْنَـٰهُ إِلَيْكَ لِتُخْرِجَ ٱلنَّاسَ مِنَ ٱلظُّلُمَـٰتِ إِلَى ٱلنُّورِ بِإِذْنِ رَبِّهِمْ - This is the magnificent book that we have sent down to you so you can pull people out of the darknesses. Which is the very purpose of the revelation
- In Arabic and other cultures, darkness is associated with difficulties, and light is associated with ease.
- And in that context, Allah mentions the story of Prophet Musa, because the Quran relies heavily on the history and case studies of the prophets.
- In this Surah, Allah starts by mentioning the Prophet's mission of pulling them out of the light, and then Allah begins the story of the Prophet Musa, so when people hear about the story, they understand that it is not just about the story of the Prophet Musa but a lesson about hope and light. Which starts in verse 5: وَلَـقَدۡ اَرۡسَلۡنَا مُوۡسٰى بِاٰيٰتِنَاۤ اَنۡ اَخۡرِجۡ قَوۡمَكَ مِنَ الظُّلُمٰتِ اِلَى النُّوۡرِ - We indeed sent Moses with Our Signs, saying: "Lead your people out of all kinds of darkness into light.
- Same message as the one for the Prophet, mentioning the similarity between Him and Musa. One being the difficult times.

---

## Surah Ibrahim, Ayah 6

- And as Allah mentions the story of the Prophet Musa, He says, وَفِىۡ ذٰ لِكُمۡ بَلَاۤ ءٌ مِّنۡ رَّبِّكُمۡ عَظِيۡمٌ - And in it there was a terrible trial from your Lord. A trial so huge that Allah himself acknowledges that.
- And after that, Allah reminds the Prophet, how Prophet Musa encouraged his people amidst such a trial, where babies were killed.

## Surah Ibrahim, Ayah 7

- Allah says, وَاِذۡ تَاَذَّنَ رَبُّكُمۡ لَٮِٕنۡ شَكَرۡتُمۡ لَاَزِيۡدَنَّـكُمۡ  - When He (Allah) let it be known, if you were grateful, I will certainly grant you more.
- Allah talks about being grateful instead of being patient, which is usually the case in times of trial. To the people who have lost their children to the enormous cruelty of Pharaoh.
- That would be the last thing on a person's mind if they had lost a child, but Allah is shifting perspective here. Because you can not be patient if you are not grateful.

---

- The **if**-part in the phrase لَٮِٕنۡ شَكَرۡتُمۡ is in the past tense; *if you **were** grateful.*
- Because in Arabic, the present tense means continuity, which means that Allah does not even demand continuous gratitude, but rather just **one** instance of gratitude.
- And in the next part, Allah tells what would happen if you showed gratitude once, لَاَزِيۡدَنَّـكُمۡ. The highest level of blessings possible, which goes beyond a possible explanation of this phrase.
- Usually, in the Quran, Allah talks in the royal, ***we***. But here Allah mentions himself in the singular form, ***I**.*
- And what is even more interesting, usually when we say increase, we have to have two words. As in who got increased and what they got increased in. Here, the ambiguity, without a qualifier for increase, shows that Allah does not want to limit the increase, hinting towards an increase in anything and everything.
- But what does being grateful mean? We have to look around and find things we can be grateful for.
- As long as we are breathing, we can be grateful, no matter what seems lost. It keeps you positive
- Then Allah says, وَلَٮِٕنۡ كَفَرۡتُمۡ اِنَّ عَذَابِىۡ لَشَدِيۡدٌ - But **if** you are ungrateful for My favors, My chastisement is terrible. Whenever we see the word **if**, we expect a **then**. Which would be expressed with a ف before اِنَّ.
- Allah mentions ***if**,* but he does not mention the word ***then***. Which means he left the statement without an effect. In this case, without the aftermath of ungratefulness.
- Allah makes it a reason for being grateful for the addressees here. That, *if you were to be grateful (for one instance), just know that my chastisement is terrible (even though I am not willing to make a direct correlation for you, out of my mercy).*
- Another point to be noted is that Allah mentions the addressees in plural, which reflects that our states of affairs can truly bring dramatic change if we influence public attitudes.

---

- If we, people, have that attitude, Allah will increase us in unity, opportunity, progress, development, and solutions.
- It reflects that the help of the unseen comes when the believer has the attitude of gratitude.
- Another point of notice is that Allah does not mention that ‘*if you are grateful to **him**’.* Which means that you have to have shukr in your heart, and it can be for anything, our parents, our teachers, our spouses, anything. And he will increase you.
- We just have to have appreciation in our lives for it to feel better

## Surah Ibrahim, Ayah 8

- وَقَالَ مُوۡسٰٓى اِنۡ تَكۡفُرُوۡۤا اَنۡـتُمۡ وَمَنۡ فِى الۡاَرۡضِ جَمِيۡعًا ۙ فَاِنَّ اللّٰهَ لَـغَنِىٌّ حَمِيۡدٌ - Moses said: "Were you to disbelieve - you and all those who live on earth - Allah is still Self-Sufficient, Innately Praiseworthy.
- Allah makes it clear that he does not need nor is he dependent on your praise or shukr.
- Allah and its deen are going to be victorious, with or without us.
- Now, this could be taken as Allah trying to express that the addressees are indispensable, but rather, here, he wants the people to know that even though Allah does not need any praise or gratitude, He is telling you to do it because He wants to give you extra credit.
- This gave hope to the people of Prophet Musa that they should be grateful that a prophet is amongst them, and hence they are being tested.
- The Sahaba of the Prophet Muhammad had the same realization, which gave them immense hope and appreciation for the Prophet's presence.
- This tells us that the bad times are not meant to stop or put us down, but rather to keep us going and make us grateful for the opportunity of a bigger mission, especially on the path of Islam.

## Gratitude = Blessings

- The Arabic word for blessing is نِعْمَة (Ni’mah), and they have two different plurals for the same word, i.e., نِعَم (ni'am) and أَنْعُم (an'um)

---

- Arabic has different grammatical numbers than English, namely, singular, pair, plural and super *awesome* plural, especially in classical Arabic (that we are targeting).
- نِعَم (ni'am) is a super *awesome* plural, and أَنْعُم (an'um) is a *weaker* plural, which means less than ten or easily countable.
- Allah uses both plurals once in the Quran.
- In **Surah Nahl (verse 121)**, Allah talks about Prophet Ibrahim, شَاكِرًا لِّاَنۡعُمِهِؕ اِجۡتَبٰٮهُ وَهَدٰٮهُ اِلٰى صِرَاطٍ مُّسۡتَقِيۡمٍ - He was grateful to Allah for a few bounties so that Allah chose him (for His favors) and directed him to the Right Way.
- Why would Allah choose a weaker plural of blessings (نِعْمَة) for Prophet Ibrahim?
- Because, prior in **Surah Nahl (verse 18)**, Allah says to Prophet Ibrahim, وَاِنۡ تَعُدُّوۡا نِعۡمَةَ اللّٰهِ لَا تُحۡصُوۡهَاؕ اِنَّ اللّٰهَ لَـغَفُوۡرٌ رَّحِيۡمٌ - For, were you to count the favor (*singular*) of Allah, you will not be able to count them. Surely Allah is Ever Forgiving, Most Merciful
- This explains that Allah mentioned that even though Prophet Ibrahim was asked to be grateful for just one favor, He was grateful for a handful of favors.
- Honoring and complimenting the Prophet Ibrahim.
- And the second time Allah uses the *super awesome* plural in **Surah Luqman (verse 20)**, saying, وَاَسۡبَغَ عَلَيۡكُمۡ نِعَمَهٗ ظَاهِرَةً وَّبَاطِنَةً ؕ - And Allah has abundantly (*uncountably excessive)* bestowed upon you all His bounties, both visible and invisible.
- Meaning all of us have been immensely, excessively blessed and even though one of the best human beings was asked to be grateful for just one favor, and when He, Allah, Himself gives, he does not give us one, a couple, or a handful. He bestows a super awesome amount of favors.
- So dare you say, you can not find anything to be grateful for? Just take a piece of paper and start noting down the blessings we have been bestowed upon, and just fully grasp at least one. We will struggle to do that.

## Conclusion

We begin our religion with the phrase اَلۡحَمۡدُ لِلّٰه (Alhamdulillah). It is a constant in our religion, which means pessimism is out of our vocabulary. Because we are the people of gratefulness (اَلۡحَمۡدُ لِلّٰه), we are always looking for the brighter side, and if that is not the case, it means we do not really understand the meaning of the phrase اَلۡحَمۡدُ لِلّٰه, which we say five times a day during salah. So whenever you are in doubt or want to do something, start with Allah’s name, بِسْمِ ٱلله, the word that brings barakah and blessings. And say, Alhamdulillah, be optimistic, and place your trust in Allah, and Allah will bless you immensely.
