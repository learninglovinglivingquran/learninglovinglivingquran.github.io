## Context

We will discuss Luqman and the wisdom Allah bestowed upon him, which he also shared with his son. Allah has dedicated a whole chapter to his name, and he is not mentioned anywhere else but in Surah Luqman. It is a consensus that Luqman is not a prophet but a righteous man endowed with حكمة (hikmah). And even though he is mentioned in the Quran as a wise man, he must have had wisdom in several aspects of his life. However, Allah chose to highlight one aspect of Luqman’s wisdom, namely how he chooses to communicate with his son and the kind of advice he gives him. 

## Luqman’s Introduction in the Quran

- Usually, when we introduce someone, we talk about their background, who they are, where they are from, and what they do, and things that make a person valuable
- But this is Allah, Himself, introducing someone, and highlighting how a human being is valued.  So Allah summarises his introduction in one ayah;

### Surah Luqman (verse 12)

وَلَقَدۡ اٰتَيۡنَا لُقۡمٰنَ الۡحِكۡمَةَ اَنِ اشۡكُرۡ لِلّٰهِؕ وَمَنۡ يَّشۡكُرۡ فَاِنَّمَا يَشۡكُرُ لِنَفۡسِهٖۚ وَمَنۡ كَفَرَ فَاِنَّ اللّٰهَ غَنِىٌّ حَمِيۡدٌ

We endowed Luqman with wisdom: ‘Be thankful to God and whoever gives thanks benefits his own soul, and as for those who are thankless––God is self-sufficient, worthy of all praise.’

### Surah Baqarah (verse 269)

- Allah says in the Quran: يُؤۡتِى الۡحِكۡمَةَ مَنۡ يَّشَآءُ ۚ وَمَنۡ يُّؤۡتَ الۡحِكۡمَةَ فَقَدۡ اُوۡتِىَ خَيۡرًا كَثِيۡرًا ؕ - and He gives wisdom to whoever He will. Whoever is given wisdom has truly been given much good
- Meaning that wisdom is a gift of Allah, and Allah focuses on mentioning the same to introduce Luqman.

## Understanding Wisdom

- When we think of wisdom, we think is someone talking in poetic language and saying profound, deep quotes.
- Or someone who is well-read, or somebody who has lived life to share their knowledge and experiences that we can learn from.
- The Arabic definition of wisdom is simple and straightforward: العِلْمُ النَّافِعُ وَالْعَمَلُ بِهِ - Beneficial knowledge is that which is acted upon.
- One could have beneficial knowledge but not act upon it, or someone could have the power to act but not have the beneficial knowledge to act upon.
- But true wisdom is to have the beneficial knowledge, which is acted upon.
- So being knowledgeable is not necessarily being wise, or being able to act does not necessarily mean someone is acting wisely.

### Knowledge that Leads to Wisdom

- Since we live in the information age, we need to be mindful of what type of knowledge we are consuming.
- We should be learning things that are beneficial to us rather than just mindlessly following people's opinions, especially on social media. That adds nothing to our lives.

---

- The knowledge that can add to wisdom is العِلْمُ النَّافِع - beneficial knowledge that adds value to our lives.
- Analyze if the knowledge that you are consuming is making you better at anything meaningful.
- And if the knowledge is beneficial, think about how you can put it to work and use that knowledge.
- And what purpose can you serve with that very knowledge?

### Luqman’s Wisdom

- Luqman has wisdom in all aspects of his life. His wisdom is like a giant, useful tree.
- But it comes from one seed, اَنِ اشۡكُرۡ لِلّٰهِ - Be grateful to Allah.
- Grammatically, the words (اَنِ اشۡكُرۡ لِلّٰهِ) represent a command from Allah.
- It explains that in any given scenario, good or bad, Luqman can have a conversation with himself and commands himself to ‘Be grateful to Allah’.
- It is not just verbal but a self-aware and heartfelt form of gratefulness that is mentioned.
- His self-corrective behavior and gratefulness are a form of profound wisdom, even mentioned by Allah to introduce him.
- Luqman’s wisdom lies in finding positive aspects in all sorts of situations and looking for opportunities to be grateful to Allah.
- Life will throw hard times at us, too, but we need to take lessons from the wisdom mentioned in the Quran.

## Wisdom for You and Me

- Allah says, وَمَنۡ يَّشۡكُرۡ فَاِنَّمَا يَشۡكُرُ لِنَفۡسِهٖۚ - and whoever gives thanks benefits his own soul.
- يَشۡكُرُ represents a continuous form of the verb. Meaning we have to be constantly grateful and pick ourselves up in the situation of distress.
- And the mindset will be beneficial to whom else but us?
- Because Allah does not need it from us.

---

- And being grateful includes appreciating the blessings you have around you.
- Your family, friends, opportunities, and everything and everyone you have benefited from in this life are blessings that need to be appreciated.
- The Prophet Muhammad [said](https://sunnah.com/abudawud:4811): “He who does not thank the people is not thankful to Allah”.
- As we go further in the ayah, Allah says, وَمَنۡ كَفَرَ - and whoever disbelieved (literal translation).
- Usually, ‘كفر’ (kufr), i.e., disbelief, is the opposite of ‘ایمان’ (emaan), i.e., faith.
- Here, ‘كفر’ (kufr) is used in opposition to ‘شكر’ (shukr).
- It explains that if we can not develop this connection with Allah and the environment around us, our belief in Allah is still weak.
- This is why the first thing Allah taught us before anything is ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّٰه, because if that is flawed, we could be missing the basics.
- And then Allah says, فَاِنَّ اللّٰهَ غَنِىٌّ حَمِيۡدٌ - God is self-sufficient, worthy of all praise
- Normally, we would expect consequences to be mentioned in response to disbelief, but rather, Allah mentions his self-sufficiency.
- It is an expression of mercy, or it could be that whoever forgets to be grateful to Allah does not get mentioned by Allah either.
- And the word حَمِيۡدٌ represents an independent noun, which can be translated to praise and thankfulness regardless of anyone or anything. Somebody who is forever praised and forever praiseworthy.

## Conclusion

And this is exactly why Luqman is wise, because he understands that he needs to be grateful to Allah. After all, Luqman himself needs it; Allah does not need it. And if he does otherwise, he will only hurt himself. And the advice he gives to his son all root from the same seed of wisdom. 

We need to be amongst the people who constantly remember Allah and thank Him, and as a result of that, [He gives us more](/topics/gratitude/references/gratitude-a-way-of-life.md) reasons to be thankful. We do not need to be the people who are drowning in negativity, and even if we are, we should come back to the word of Allah. Allah’s word can heal us and restore us, and we can go back to being in our grateful state.
