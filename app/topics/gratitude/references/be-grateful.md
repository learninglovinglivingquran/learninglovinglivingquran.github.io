## Surah Ar-Rehman (verse 31-34)

سَنَفۡرُغُ لَـكُمۡ اَيُّهَ الثَّقَلٰنِۚ **٣١** فَبِاَىِّ اٰلَاۤءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبٰنِ **٣٢** يٰمَعۡشَرَ الۡجِنِّ وَالۡاِنۡسِ اِنِ اسۡتَطَعۡتُمۡ اَنۡ تَنۡفُذُوۡا مِنۡ اَقۡطَارِ السَّمٰوٰتِ وَالۡاَرۡضِ فَانْفُذُوۡاؕ لَا تَنۡفُذُوۡنَ اِلَّا بِسُلۡطٰنٍۚ **٣٣** فَبِاَىِّ اٰلَاۤءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبٰنِ **٣٤**

We shall attend to you, two huge armies [of jinn and mankind], **31** Which, then, of your Lord’s blessings do you both deny? **32** Jinn and mankind, if you can pass beyond the regions of heaven and earth, then do so: you will not pass without Our authority. **33** Which, then, of your Lord’s blessings do you both deny? **34** 

## Context

- The focus in this khutbah (sermon) is mainly going to be on this one scene described on judgment day from Surah Ar-Rehman.
- The word Ar-Rehman is one of the powerful names of Allah that depicts Allah’s care and mercy, that blesses without any strings attached.
- He (Allah) even blesses those who do not care for Him or his deen.
- In the times when everyone is obsessed with themselves and the material around them, ignoring what they have been blessed with, it makes it difficult to be grateful.
- People often focus so much on what they do not have and ignore the blessings of Allah, including necessities, fresh air, food, water, and shelter.
- Such people are so negative that they are never really appreciative of the gifts, gifts of parents, health, and time.
- If we do not fight it during the times of youth, it gets worse and results in greed. This is actually the reason behind wars.
- Just saying ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّٰه, recognizing the Ar-Rehman (the Most Merciful and the Most Gracious), and appreciating the blessings bestowed upon one can actually save humankind from much bigger problems.
- If each of us plays our part, we can bring a positive change in society by maintaining a balance between taking care of ourselves and appreciating what we have.

## Scenes from the Day of Judgment

- Now, as we are heading towards the scenes from the judgment day, just imagine when a teacher pulls a student out from the class, what happens to the rest of the class? They all go in a state of shock, even though only one of them is singled out.
- Allah is telling the human beings to be grateful, each one of them.
- But when you are ungrateful and defy Allah, He does not punish you or single you out, except it is not going to happen yet.

### Verse 31

- Allah says, سَنَفۡرُغُ لَـكُمۡ اَيُّهَ الثَّقَلٰنِۚ - We shall attend to you, two huge armies [of jinn and mankind], as in, We will make a lot of free time, especially for you two groups.
- Here, the word الثَّقَلٰنِۚ is used because people might think that there are way too many to be addressed or be seen by Allah, but Allah makes it clear that He sees each one of us.

### Verse 32

- And He says, فَبِاَىِّ اٰلَاۤءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبٰنِ - Which, then, of your Lord’s blessings do you both deny? As in you, how many favors of Allah are you still willing to deny?

### Verse 33

- To understand this ayah (verse), let's imagine a scene. Cops running after a criminal, and suddenly a police officer stops and tells the criminal to run a little faster. Why would that be?
- Only if the cop knew that the criminal was already surrounded.

---

- There could be people who must have a very ignorant attitude towards Allah, and then the day of judgment comes.
- The sky starts ripping, the earth starts shaking, and mountains start flying, and they realize that it's time to stand trial in front of Allah. So they (jinns and mankind). start to decide to run away from it.
- But they can only run between the earth and sky (since the jinns can fly).

### Verse 34

- And Allah repeats, فَبِاَىِّ اٰلَاۤءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبٰنِ
- How much more ungrateful could you be to the blessings of your Rab (Lord).

## Conclusion

Surah Ar-Rehman is mainly focused on one idea, that is, the idea of getting across the seriousness to the people who are ungrateful to Allah. Those who do not thank Allah enough and do not appreciate what they have been given. That is why the question keeps coming, “*Which, then, of your Lord’s blessings do you both deny?*” So if you want to accomplish great things in life, be grateful. Allah says, لَٮِٕنۡ شَكَرۡتُمۡ لَاَزِيۡدَنَّـكُمۡ - If you were grateful, Allah will grant you more ([Surah Ibrahim, Ayah 7](/topics/gratitude/references/gratitude-a-way-of-life.md). 

To start with, let us start being grateful to our parents, they are one of the greatest gifts of Allah to you. Let us appreciate all the things they have ever done for us, from allowing us to be healthy and providing for us to teaching us lessons of life.

May Allah make every single one of us grateful and not amongst those who would be running on the day of judgment. May Allah make us amongst those who are protected by the shade of Allah.
