## **Surah Saba (Verse 13)**

يَعۡمَلُوۡنَ لَهٗ مَا يَشَآءُ مِنۡ مَّحَارِيۡبَ وَتَمَاثِيۡلَ وَجِفَانٍ كَالۡجَـوَابِ وَقُدُوۡرٍ رّٰسِيٰتٍ ؕ اِعۡمَلُوۡۤا اٰلَ دَاوٗدَ شُكۡرًا ؕ وَقَلِيۡلٌ مِّنۡ عِبَادِىَ الشَّكُوۡرُ

## Context

They made him whatever he wanted- palaces, statues, basins as large as water troughs, fixed cauldrons. We said, ‘Work thankfully, family of Daud, for a few of my servants are truly thankful.

- Sharing some reflections on the concept of شكر (shukr) (gratitude)
- The meaning of shukr is already embedded in the word حمد (hamd), which we recite in Surah Al-Fatiha.
- The meanings of shukr are part of the meanings of hamd. Making shukr a subset of hamd.
- Hamd includes praise and a very particular kind of attitude.
- Allah mentions the concept of shukr in the Quran, where a father is assuring a good upbringing for his son.
- Allah says, وَلَقَدۡ اٰتَيۡنَا لُقۡمٰنَ الۡحِكۡمَةَ اَنِ اشۡكُرۡ لِلّٰهِؕ - We endowed Luqman with wisdom: ‘Be grateful to Allah.’
- Allah gave Luqman the wisdom, the summary of which was being grateful to Allah. And that is what Luqman teaches his son.
- We think of shukr as something *passive*, something we do in return for a favor.
- However, the Quran also gives a second side of shukr, which we will discuss further.
- We shall start with the dua that Allah mentions in the Quran, of a man reaching the age of 40.

### Surah Ahqaf (Verse 15)

- ؕ حَتّٰٓى اِذَا بَلَغَ اَشُدَّهٗ وَبَلَغَ اَرۡبَعِيۡنَ سَنَةً - Until when he reaches his maturity and reaches forty years.
- 40 is an important age in the Quran and Arabic literature, because a man comes to a source of maturity at the age of 40, settles socially and economically
- At 40, your horizon becomes bigger, and you can think and see other things that you would not, a couple of years ago.
- Allah further quotes the dua of a mature man in the ayah; سَنَةً  ۙ قَالَ رَبِّ اَوۡزِعۡنِىۡۤ اَنۡ اَشۡكُرَ نِعۡمَتَكَ الَّتِىۡۤ اَنۡعَمۡتَ عَلَىَّ وَعَلٰى وَالِدَىَّ وَاَنۡ اَعۡمَلَ صَالِحًا تَرۡضٰٮهُ وَاَصۡلِحۡ لِىۡ فِىۡ ذُرِّيَّتِىۡ ؕۚ اِنِّىۡ تُبۡتُ اِلَيۡكَ وَاِنِّىۡ مِنَ الۡمُسۡلِمِيۡنَ - ‘Oh Lord empower me to be truly grateful for your favors to me and to my parents; help me to do good work that pleases You; make my offspring good. I turn to You; I am one of those who devote themselves to You.’

### Surah An-Naml (Verse 19)

- The first half of the dua is again mentioned by the Prophet Suleiman, in the same **exact** words: رَبِّ اَوۡزِعۡنِىۡۤ اَنۡ اَشۡكُرَ نِعۡمَتَكَ الَّتِىۡۤ اَنۡعَمۡتَ عَلَىَّ وَعَلٰى وَالِدَىَّ وَاَنۡ اَعۡمَلَ صَالِحًا تَرۡضٰٮهُ - ‘Oh Lord, empower me to be truly grateful for your favors to me and to my parents; help me to do good work that pleases You.’
- Why is Allah teaching us the dua that is echoing from 1000s of years ago, before the Quran, on the tongue of a great Prophet?
- Prophet Daud and Suleiman are the exceptional prophets because, unlike most prophets, they were in a position of power and strength.

---

- Allah describes in Surah Saba some of what both the Prophets were doing.

### Surah Saba (Verse 11)

- اَنِ اعۡمَلۡ سٰبِغٰتٍ وَّقَدِّرۡ فِى السَّرۡدِ وَاعۡمَلُوۡا صَالِحًـا ؕ اِنِّىۡ بِمَا تَعۡمَلُوۡنَ بَصِيۡرٌ - (Daud) saying (to Suleiman), ‘Make sheets of chain mail and measure the links well.’ (And Allah says) ‘Do good, all of you, for I see everything you do.’

### Surah Saba (Verse 13)

- And then Allah mentions a remarkable thing; يَعۡمَلُوۡنَ لَهٗ مَا يَشَآءُ مِنۡ مَّحَارِيۡبَ وَتَمَاثِيۡلَ وَجِفَانٍ كَالۡجَـوَابِ وَقُدُوۡرٍ رّٰسِيٰتٍ ؕ اِعۡمَلُوۡۤا اٰلَ دَاوٗدَ شُكۡرًا ؕ وَقَلِيۡلٌ مِّنۡ عِبَادِىَ الشَّكُوۡرُ - They (jinns) made him whatever he wanted- palaces, statues, basins as large as water troughs, fixed cauldrons. We said, ‘Do shukr (gratefulness), family of David, and for a few of my servants are truly grateful.’
- Allah is not telling the family of Daud to say shukr, but to do shukr, and what does that mean?
- And Allah ends the verse by saying, ‘and for a few of my servants are truly grateful’.
- Means that gratitude to Allah is more than just lip service and reaction.
- Allah is inspiring the mentioned prophets to use the resources, melt them, and build and produce something.
- And the act of building (the factories) is going to go beyond them.
- Which means that Allah wants us to know that if we use the resources at our disposal and make the most of them for a cause, it is an act of shukr.
- And this concept of shukr is the *other* form of shukr (not passive as we mentioned earlier).

- And what does Allah promise if we do shukr?

- Allah says, لَٮِٕنۡ شَكَرۡتُمۡ لَاَزِيۡدَنَّـكُمۡ -  if you were grateful, I will certainly grant you more.
- Allah promises to increase you more, increase whatever you have.

## Conclusion

This study is not just about the prosperity of the human being or a muslim ted talk. But it is about the نِعْمَة (Ni’mah) and the أمانة (Amanah) that Allah has given us, i.e., the blessings and the trust that Allah has granted upon us. It is about the money, energy, resources, and everything that Allah has given us, and put it to maximum use to produce the most amount of benefit. And while doing all of that, rather than boasting about it, since we can not be bigger than Dhul Qarnain anyway, we should be grateful to Allah. And even Dhul Qarnain, being as powerful as he was, in the end of it all, praises Allah and acknowledges that it is all in Allah’s will.

### Surah Kahf (Verse 98)

As Dhul Qarnain says: قَالَ هٰذَا رَحۡمَةٌ مِّنۡ رَّبِّىۡ ۚ فَاِذَا جَآءَ وَعۡدُ رَبِّىۡ جَعَلَهٗ دَكَّآءَ ۚ وَكَانَ وَعۡدُ رَبِّىۡ حَقًّا ؕ - and he said, ‘This is a mercy from my Lord. But when my Lord’s promise is fulfilled, He will raze this barrier to the ground: my Lord’s promise always comes true.’

Muslims often talk about how building and getting the most out of our resources is not needed, since they want to be grateful for what they have. And everything is going to come to an end.  But if that was the case, why would Allah be telling Prophet Daud and his offspring to build and do shukr? Because that is our passive mentality and has nothing to do with the teachings of the Quran. And the mindset guided here can actually take a nation from weakness to strength. 

And since the message is about using our blessings for positive change through gratitude, let us look into the [Hadith](https://sunnah.com/bukhari:6011) of the Prophet Muhammad, where He compared the Ummah (Muslim Nation) to one body. And imagine one part of the body is sick and the person is not willing to make it better, then it is not actually the part of the body that is sick, but the mind. But when a person’s heart and mind are in the right place, they can still be sick, and they can be healthy and continue to get better. So if the Prophet compares the Ummah to a body and the Ummah is weak, we need to change now. And as we bring any change to the body, it reacts to it and sustains a planned and gradual progress. And any change we bring to the body simultaneously, it collapses. And hence, when it comes to the youth, it is high time we start to strengthen ourselves, change our mindsets, and work to make this one body stronger and better. And when it comes to the older people of the Ummah, it is time to settle and be grateful to Allah by helping the youth build, invest in them, teach them and help the body get strong and better.
