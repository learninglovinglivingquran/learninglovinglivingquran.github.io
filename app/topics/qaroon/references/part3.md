## Context

This is the third and final part of the story about an individual, Qaroon, who is from the time of Prophet Musa (Moses). And it is to be noted that the stories in the Quran aren't just stories but archetypes. Allah tells us about only a handful of stories because whenever we are told a story in the Quran, it is because it will be repeated in our lives, and we can learn something from it. And in this story, we get to learn from Qaroon and the advice that he had been given by the people around him and by Allah.

## Final Advice to Qaroon

### Surah Al-Qasas (verse 77)

وَلَا تَبۡغِ الۡـفَسَادَ فِى الۡاَرۡضِؕ اِنَّ اللّٰهَ لَا يُحِبُّ الۡمُفۡسِدِيۡنَ

Do not seek to spread corruption in the land, for God does not love those who do this

- The individual talked about here is clearly better off than others around him, and he likes to show off, as we have discussed earlier.
- And when you spend frivolously and show off, it becomes less about yourself and more about other people.
- It sets a wrong standard and spreads corruption by making people wish upon the things that stand out.
- On social media, influencers, celebrities, and even regular people curate perfect lives and share everything with the world.
- People share their money, vacation, children, relationships, etc. Completely ignoring how they might make somebody feel desperate.
- People around feel a constant sense of comparison falling into the pit of ingratitude.
- But our religion starts with ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّٰهِ, i.e., being grateful and praising Allah.
- The concept of ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّٰهِ is not just a verbal practice, but believing that Allah is enough and being grateful for the blessings bestowed upon us.
- But the sense of missing out all the time, and constant comparison, can not really let you be grateful.
- Therefore, there is no need to share everything online and spread corruption amongst the people. Instead, we should be grateful to Allah as well.

### Surah Ad-Duha (verse 10-11)

**11** وَاَمَّا السَّآٮِٕلَ فَلَا تَنۡهَرۡؕ **10** وَاَمَّا بِنِعۡمَةِ رَبِّكَ فَحَدِّثۡ 

and do not chide the one who asks for help, **10** talk about the blessings of your Lord. **11**

- In fact, Allah says to talk about your blessings to help people. Which means to talk them out of dark times and give them hope and motivation to restore their faith in good times and gratitude.

## Israf and Its Effects

- Prophet Muhammad (Peace be Upon Him) liked dressing well and eating good food. So living a good life is never discouraged in Islam, but Israf (extravagance) is strictly prohibited.

### Surah Al-Isra (verse 26-27)

وَلَا تُبَذِّرۡ تَبۡذِيۡرًا **26** اِنَّ الۡمُبَذِّرِيۡنَ كَانُوۡۤا اِخۡوَانَ الشَّيٰطِيۡنِ ؕ وَكَانَ الشَّيۡطٰنُ لِرَبِّهٖ كَفُوۡرًا **27**

Do not squander your wealth wastefully: those who squander are the brothers of Satan, and Satan is most ungrateful to his Lord

- Allah calls the wasteful spenders, the brothers of Shaitan (Satan, who is the most *ungrateful*.
- Being *ungrateful* is the real disease that Shaitan instills in us by constantly following new trends, newer games, and fashion, and never truly being happy with anything.
- And when this sickness happens in Muslims, the urge to constantly consume and show off, there remains no difference between us and people who do not believe.
- Because if someone is just chasing after this life, trying to make it a heaven, then why would one care about the afterlife? They could still have a muslim name or practice rituals, but the mindset is the same as that of a non-believer.

**Surah Al-Mumtahinah (verse 5)**

رَبَّنَا لَا تَجۡعَلۡنَا فِتۡنَةً لِّلَّذِيۡنَ كَفَرُوۡا

Our Lord, do not make us a test for the unbelievers

- We are taught a dua for us not to become a test to the disbelievers, when it would be expected otherwise.
- Because if we fail to live our lives according to the teachings of the Quran, there is not going to be any difference between them and us. And what would lead them to enter Islam then?
- What greater fitnah would there be if the people who believe in monotheism, in the Prophet Muhammad (Peace be Upon Him) and his message, display nothing of their faith and belief? And their mentality is like anybody else, and they think of nobody or nothing other than themselves.
- In this world of social media and sharing everything, we have to be mindful of what we are leaving behind and its impact on the world.

## Qaroon’s Response

### Surah Al-Qasas (verse 78)

قَالَ اِنَّمَاۤ اُوۡتِيۡتُهٗ عَلٰى عِلۡمٍ عِنۡدِىۡؕ

He replied: "All this has been given to me on account of a certain knowledge that I have.”

- When someone believes that they are blessed because of themselves, their actions, academics, hard work, etc. They forget that there are people much more talented than they are who are not as blessed as they are, and there are people who lack talent and yet are blessed enormously.
- Meaning Allah is the one who blesses people with whatever they have, and no external or personal means can change that.
- Also, Qaroon did not respond to the problem because Allah never said he (Qaroon) could not possess wealth, but he was told not to show off, and he deflected the topic in arrogance.

## Allah’s Response to Qaroon

اَوَلَمۡ يَعۡلَمۡ اَنَّ اللّٰهَ قَدۡ اَهۡلَكَ مِنۡ قَبۡلِهٖ مِنَ الۡقُرُوۡنِ مَنۡ هُوَ اَشَدُّ مِنۡهُ قُوَّةً وَّاَكۡثَرُ جَمۡعًاؕ وَلَا يُسۡــَٔلُ عَنۡ ذُنُوۡبِهِمُ الۡمُجۡرِمُوۡنَ

Did he not know that Allah had destroyed before him those who were stronger in might than he and were more numerous in multitude? The wicked are not asked about their acts of sin.

- Allah dislikes his arrogance so much that He (Allah) responds with the destruction of *nations* (plural) in this world, to this *single* individual.
- Allah expresses His anger so much that He tells Qaroon, in response to his arrogance, that the criminals (the spreaders of corruption) won’t even be asked anything before they are destroyed (in the afterlife).
- And since Qaroon is from the Israelites, he was a believer. And this is Allah’s lesson to a *believer*. And he got punished in this life.

## Summary

Qaroon is a scary case, since he was punished and made an example for us in this world. But before it happens, before we are presented before Allah in an arrogant state, we need to be cautious. We need to see the bigger picture and not get blinded by the fake status quo, the race of likes and dislikes, and the fake world of perfection. Because people that you do all this for do not really like or love you, they are all going to be running away from you on the day of judgment, trying to save themselves.

We need to be authentic, grateful, and be mindful of our reactions and their reactions on this world. May Allah give us strength to become the people of reality and goodness and save us from the facade and corruption.
