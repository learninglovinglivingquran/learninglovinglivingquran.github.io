## Context

The Quran mentions a person in the story of the Prophet Musa, named Qaroon. Qaroon is from the nation of Israelites, so that he could be a distant relative of Prophet Musa. When Pharaoh kept his people as slaves, Qaroon was a spy amongst the captives, making tons of money by spying on his people. To help the rulers avoid any internal riot or rebellion.

The Quran mentions Qaroon in; 

### Surah Al-Qassas (verse 76)

إِنَّ قَٰرُونَ كَانَ مِن قَوۡمِ مُوسَىٰ فَبَغَىٰ عَلَيۡهِمۡۖ وَءَاتَيۡنَٰهُ مِنَ ٱلۡكُنُوزِ مَآ إِنَّ مَفَاتِحَهُۥ لَتَنُوٓأُ بِٱلۡعُصۡبَةِ أُوْلِي ٱلۡقُوَّةِ إِذۡ قَالَ لَهُۥ قَوۡمُهُۥ لَا تَفۡرَحۡۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ ٱلۡفَرِحِينَ

Qaroon was one of Moses’ people, but he oppressed them. We had given him such treasures that even their keys would have weighed down a whole company of strong men. His people said to him, ‘Do not gloat, for God does not like people who gloat.

- Qaroon had so much money that he had servants to carry the keys to his treasures.
- لَتَنُوٓأُ بِٱلۡعُصۡبَةِ أُوْلِي ٱلۡقُوَّةِ means that a group of strong men would get tired (carrying his treasure keys).
- The verse is not just about Qaroon but such type of people.
- Even though Allah has mentioned a sell-out in this verse but the lesson to be taken from this verse is the advice that he was given here

## First Advice to Qaroon

- The first advice that he was given is; إِذۡ قَالَ لَهُۥ قَوۡمُهُۥ لَا تَفۡرَحۡۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ ٱلۡفَرِحِينَ
- The literal translation is: ‘Do not be happy, for God does not like people who are happy’.
- But the word **فرح** (the base word of تَفۡرَحۡۖ and ٱلۡفَرِحِينَ) means to be overjoyed by laughter.
- Meaning that Allah forbids getting overpowered by the joy of money, and chasing endless exciting highs.

### Surah Al-Qassas (verse 79)

فَخَرَجَ عَلٰى قَوۡمِهٖ فِىۡ زِيۡنَتِهٖؕ قَالَ الَّذِيۡنَ يُرِيۡدُوۡنَ الۡحَيٰوةَ الدُّنۡيَا يٰلَيۡتَ لَـنَا مِثۡلَ مَاۤ اُوۡتِىَ قَارُوۡنُۙ اِنَّهٗ لَذُوۡ حَظٍّ عَظِيۡمٍ

He went out among his people in all his pomp, and those whose aim was the life of this world said, ‘If only we had been given something like what Qaroon has been given: he really is a very fortunate man,’

- In simple words, Allah forbids showing off and becoming heedless to your fun
- However, we now have many Qaroons among us who like to show off and share everything on social media to impress others and gain admiration.
- But that is a fake world and far from the purpose of this life.
- The verses do not mean that you should not be happy.
- Rather, people who chase one eventful high after another are actually trying to fill the void of sadness inside themselves.
- But running from the ugly truth does not help, hence the higher rates of suicide amongst more successful individuals are noticed ([Research](https://sc.mp/2tuYbjg)).

### Surah Al-Qassas (verse 77)

- وَابۡتَغِ فِيۡمَاۤ اٰتٰٮكَ اللّٰهُ الدَّارَ الۡاٰخِرَةَ - Seek the life to come by means of what God has granted you
- Allah has blessed us with a halal (permissible) avenue for everything that we need in this life, and He wants us to enjoy the things that are made halal for us.
- Allah, who is the Lord of mankind, knows that the things that are made halal for humans are actually better.
- And on the other hand, the act of enjoying halal, itself, is an act of worship.
- Earning halal, eating halal, and shopping halal not only gives us happiness in this world but is also an act of worship that benefits the afterlife as well.
- People might call it a boring or sad way to live life, but Allah Almighty, Himself, continues in the same verse, وَلَا تَنۡسَ نَصِيۡبَكَ مِنَ الدُّنۡيَا - but do not neglect your rightful share in this world.
- Allah has bestowed us with all the blessings, He has mentioned being in Jannah, e.g., birds, trees, servants, rivers, fruits, etc.
- He (Allah) has made this world a preview of Jannah (heavens) for us to enjoy rightfully, so we can be grateful.

### Surah Al-Baqarah (verse 168)

- Allah says; يٰٓاَيُّهَا النَّاسُ كُلُوۡا مِمَّا فِى الۡاَرۡضِ حَلٰلًا طَيِّبًا وَّلَا تَتَّبِعُوۡا خُطُوٰتِ الشَّيۡطٰنِؕ - People, eat what is good and lawful from the earth, and do not follow Satan’s footsteps
- When Allah describes halal, he also calls it طَيِّب (tayyib), which means pure.
- It means that everything that is not halal is not only bad for us but also brings corruption and problems into our lives.

## Summary

It is a reminder that life is very short and we have something to show before Allah. And, we did not come into this world to chase endless highs and fun, but rather a much bigger mission of doing good. And if we miss the opportunity of using our resources and capabilities to do good, we are actually missing the responsibility that Allah has put on us. We need to internalize this lesson and ask ourselves, what good am I going to leave in this world? How much of my thoughts are just about myself and my benefit, and how much of it is used for other people? Are we the people of benefit? And we do not need to do it for hundreds or thousands, but even if it is a single person today who can benefit from us, we should be doing it! May Allah make us amongst those.
