 إِنَّ قَـٰرُونَ كَانَ مِن قَوْمِ مُوسَىٰ فَبَغَىٰ عَلَيْهِمْ ۖ وَءَاتَيْنَـٰهُ مِنَ ٱلْكُنُوزِ مَآ إِنَّ مَفَاتِحَهُۥ لَتَنُوٓأُ بِٱلْعُصْبَةِ أُو۟لِى ٱلْقُوَّةِ إِذْ قَالَ لَهُۥ قَوْمُهُۥ لَا تَفْرَحْ ۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ ٱلْفَرِحِينَ **76**

وَٱبْتَغِ فِيمَآ ءَاتَىٰكَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلدَّارَ ٱلْـَٔاخِرَةَ ۖ وَلَا تَنسَ نَصِيبَكَ مِنَ ٱلدُّنْيَا ۖ وَأَحْسِن كَمَآ أَحْسَنَ ٱللَّهُ إِلَيْكَ ۖ وَلَا تَبْغِ ٱلْفَسَادَ فِى ٱلْأَرْضِ ۖ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ ٱلْمُفْسِدِينَ **77**

Qarun was one of Moses’ people, but he oppressed them. We had given him such treasures that even their keys would have weighed down a whole company of strong men. His people said to him, ‘Do not gloat, for God does not like people who gloat. **76**

Seek the life to come by means of what God has granted you, but do not neglect your rightful share in this world. Do good to others as God has done good to you. Do not seek to spread corruption in the land, for God does not love those who do this.’ **77**

## Context

In part 1, we discussed the advice that Allah gave Qaroon in these ayahs. The main one being, to not go heedless in the chase of constant highs. This is a lesson for ourselves to be mindful of our blessings and resources and make better use of them, since everything is an أمانة (Amanah). Because when people are comfortable and their needs are met, we pay no attention to their purpose and fall short on being grateful. Even if something seems religious, and the intention behind it is to just have fun and party and backbite in the gatherings, there is no point to it.

## Second Advice to Qaroon

وَابۡتَغِ فِيمَآ ءَاتَىٰكَ ٱللَّهُ ٱلدَّارَ ٱلْـَٔاخِرَةَ - Seek the life to come by means of what God has granted you

- Which means that every living moment, the act we do is an opportunity to get something in life and the afterlife, from what Allah has given us
- We should try more and seek what we can do in this life that can also benefit our next life. Invest in the *real* future
- It is like transferring money into a bank account that you know is yours, but you can not touch it yet. But it will eventually benefit you.
- Whatever we have in this world will eventually be gone, or if it is not gone, we shall be gone.
- So it is better we spend on charity, and do bigger good. Spend our time, energy, and resources on beneficial work.

---

- Through this advice, Allah wants us to have the ability to think ahead. And take a moment and think if our actions are beneficial for our future, or even the present.
- That is why we are taught the dua (prayer); رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ - Our Lord, grant us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire. (Surah Al-Baqarah, ayah 202)
- Because if we do good in this world, we automatically build the akhirah (afterlife), and Islam does not separate one from another.

## Third Advice to Qaroon

- When we talk about the afterlife, somebody might think that we will have to miss out on this life.
- But Allah says, وَلَا تَنسَ نَصِيبَكَ مِنَ ٱلدُّنْيَا - but do not neglect your rightful share in this world
- Which means that not only does Allah want us to have our rightful share in our lives, but it is our duty to take care of ourselves and what we have been blessed with.
- Take care of our body, our health, our mind, our surroundings, and mental peace. And it becomes easier to do so with the laws and limits that Allah has set for us.
- Allah does not want us to be miserable and sacrifice our dignity and happiness, but enjoy our fair share of life within limits.
- Nobody should have to tolerate abuse, humiliation, and sacrifice in the name of sabr (patience), because Allah does not even want it for Himself.
- And you can not put anybody above Allah’s law and let shaitan come in. So do not let anybody the rights that Allah has given you.
- Respectfully take your rights in this life and abide by Allah’s law. And when you are doing all that, take care of the next portion of this advice: وَأَحْسِن كَمَآ أَحْسَنَ ٱللَّهُ إِلَيْكَ - Do good to others as God has done good to you.
- One of the translations of the word اَحْسِن is ‘to do something beautifully’.
- So it this part of the verse could mean, *do it beautifully to others as Allah has done it beautifully to you.*
- Meaning that we should have a positive attitude and think about the environment, mind, money, help, opportunities, and all the gifts that Allah has given us.
- Even if you are in dark times, be grateful that you can bring light into the darkness.

---

- But to be able to appreciate the things around you and do good, first you have to take care of yourself.
- Charity is not just about money, but it could also be about saying something nice with your tongue, having a nice attitude, and gestures towards people.
- You could become a source of joy and not misery for other people, because Allah has been good to us.
- In simpler words, Allah is telling us that since Allah is the best to us, we need to be the best at everything we do in our lives. Our academics, work, relationships, how we carry ourselves, and how we deal with people, all should reflect our best behavior because we have been blessed with the best.
- But shaitan (devil) wants us to be blind to what Allah has bestowed us with and focus on our problems and blame them on Allah, and complain.

## Qaroon’s Punishment

### Surah Al Qasas (verse 78)

قَالَ اِنَّمَاۤ اُوۡتِيۡتُهٗ عَلٰى عِلۡمٍ عِنۡدِىۡؕ اَوَلَمۡ يَعۡلَمۡ اَنَّ اللّٰهَ قَدۡ اَهۡلَكَ مِنۡ قَبۡلِهٖ مِنَ الۡقُرُوۡنِ مَنۡ هُوَ اَشَدُّ مِنۡهُ قُوَّةً وَّاَكۡثَرُ جَمۡعًاؕ

but he answered, ‘This wealth was given to me on account of the knowledge I possess.’ Did he not know that God had destroyed many generations before him, who had greater power than him and built up greater wealth? 

- Allah mentions the *nations* that were destroyed before him, while he was just one man. And Allah buried him in this life just as he destroyed those nations.
- So if we do not change our behavior and kill the Qaroon inside us, it is not far that we shall become one and be eligible to the punishment in this life before we see anything in the next.

## Summary

Allah wants us to focus on our blessings and make the most of them to be beneficial for ourselves and our future and be a source of goodness to others, just as Allah is a source of all good in our lives. And if we can do that, we can save ourselves from becoming Qaroon. Allah mentions people like Qaroon to us so we learn from them and do not become one.

We can stop ourselves, our family, and friends from becoming one such person by being more aware of how we spend our time, and how we can not just entertain ourselves, but also think about the real future and become a source of goodness. May Allah protect us and our generations from such a curse and not make us believe that this life is all there is.
